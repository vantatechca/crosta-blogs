Cold fermentation is the quiet engine behind a great crust. Over 72 hours in the fridge, the yeast works slowly while enzymes break starches into simpler sugars. The payoff is a base that's lighter, more digestible, and tastes of more than just flour.

At Pizzeria Crosta we mix on Monday for a Thursday bake. The dough comes out blistered and open-crumbed, with that faint sourness you only get from time.
